# Purpose
This skill helps Codex explain Sales Data OS consistently as a business operating framework, not just a technical build.

Use it whenever the task involves describing the user's flagship project or turning project details into a strong business narrative.

# Project definition
Sales Data OS is an integrated sales operations framework that unifies fragmented sales, target, activity, and management data under one operating logic so that managers can diagnose current status, understand causes, and decide next actions.

# What it solves
The project addresses a common problem in sales organizations:
performance is often managed as a final result rather than as a controllable process.

Sales Data OS is designed to shift management from:
- result checking
to
- process diagnosis, coaching, and action prioritization

# Core message
The most important message is:
Sales Data OS turns field sales management into a more scientific operating model by connecting performance, behavior, and management action.

# What it is not
Do not frame it as:
- just a dashboard
- only a reporting screen
- a generic BI copy
- an AI gimmick
- a finished commercial SaaS unless explicitly requested

# Key business components
Use these as business components, not technical jargon.

## KPI logic
The system combines official performance indicators and behavior-based indicators so managers can understand not just what happened, but why it happened.

Relevant examples:
- HIR
- RTR
- BCR

These should be framed as behavior-based KPI logic used to identify drivers of performance.

## Comparative operating view
The system helps compare team, branch, and regional conditions under common standards.

Business value:
- identify gaps
- find priority areas
- support fairer diagnosis
- reduce management by intuition alone

## Coaching support
The system is designed so managers can move from data reading to coaching action.

Business value:
- clearer coaching points
- practical intervention scenarios
- action-focused reporting

## Reporting transformation
Analysis is converted into decision-ready reporting output rather than raw data display.

Business value:
- faster management communication
- better alignment
- easier reporting rhythm

# Main modules
Describe modules by the business question they answer.

## Territory
Business meaning:
Helps analyze regional / institution coverage and define territory optimization standards.

## Prescription
Business meaning:
Tracks performance flow by product, institution, and representative to identify shifts and inflection points.

## Reporting
Business meaning:
Turns analysis results into structured reports that support management decision making.

# Writing rules
## Business-first explanation
Start with why the project matters to managers and organizations.
Only mention implementation detail if needed.

## Decision-oriented description
Every description should answer:
What can a manager see, understand, or decide because of this?

## No feature dumping
Do not list screens or functions without explaining management meaning.

## Action linkage
Always connect:
data → diagnosis → insight → action

## Practical tone
Keep the tone realistic, useful, and grounded in field-sales management.

# Preferred structure
Unless the user asks otherwise, explain the project in this order:
1. problem
2. project definition
3. management logic
4. key modules / structures
5. business value
6. future direction or role relevance

# Good phrasing
Use phrasing like:
- integrates fragmented sales data into one management logic
- helps managers diagnose status under common standards
- links activity, target, and result into a controllable operating rhythm
- supports coaching and reporting, not just passive monitoring
- identifies performance drivers rather than showing final numbers only
- turns field complexity into decision-ready management views

# Avoid
Avoid:
- AI-powered future of sales
- revolutionary platform
- smart dashboard solution
- digital transformation buzzwords without meaning
- overly technical architecture explanation unless explicitly requested
